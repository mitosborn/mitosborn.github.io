Rice University

Data Science Researcher

Houston, TX May 2020 - April 2021

As a researcher in Dr. Sylvia Dee's Climate Lab, I investigated the environmental impacts of the COVID-19 lockdown on Texas. To do this, I utilized statistical modelling on historical data to predict what environmental indicator levels would have been if the lockdown didn't occur in each of Texas' 254 counties. I then divided the data into three partitions (pre-lockdown, lockdown, and post-lockdown) and performed significance testing. I found that the reductions in pollutants from the lockdown were mostly cancelled out by the effects of reopening.

To best communicate these results, I built a web dashboard utilizing the Python package Dash. This dashboard utilizes an interactive heat-map, time series plot, and scatter plot to visualize trends of eight environmental and two socioeconomic indicators throughout the lockdown.

To complete this work, I worked closely with a diverse team of environmental science, computer science, and statistics majors. The results of this research and the subsequent dashboard were presented at the American Geophysical Union conference. While the paper is in the process of being published.

COVID-19 Environmental Impacts Dashboard

Rice Climate Laboratory

Spring 2020 - April 2021 | Dashboard | Github

COVID-19 Environmental Impacts Dashboard
Previous Next

Description: Year long project to assess environmental and socioeconomic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic on Texas. I worked in a team of 8 students of various majors to design and implement a data science pipeline to assess COVID's impacts. This pipeline collected the most recent environmental and socioeconomic county level data, compared historical and present data using statistics and time-series modelling, and visualized impacts by metropolitan area and county with a dashboard.

To implement this pipeline, I first utilized Selenium to automate downloading historical data from the Texas Comission for Environmental Quality (TCEQ) due to their lack of an API. Then, I leveraged Python's Pandas and Statsmodels packages to group data by county and metropolitan area and model what indicator levels would have been if the pandemic never occurred. Finally, to improve interpretability and simplify analysis of the data, I created an interactive dashboard using Dash, a Python dashboarding package.

The results of this research and the subsequent dashboard were presented at the American Geophysical Union conference. Furthermore, the paper is in the process of being published.

Packages used: Dash, Plotly, Matplotlib, Statsmodels, Pandas, Selenium.
